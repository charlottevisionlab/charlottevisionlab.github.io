Vision systems that understand people, scenes, and motion.

Charlotte Vision Lab is a research-driven hub at UNC Charlotte advancing the frontiers of visual intelligence. Our work spans video understanding, multimodal learning, robotic perception, generative modeling, and trustworthy machine vision, unified by a mission to build systems that see, reason, and act in complex real-world environments.

About

A shared vision for reliable, multimodal perception.

Charlotte Vision Lab combines modern machine learning, geometry, multimodal reasoning, and embodied intelligence to study how visual systems can generalize in open, real-world settings.

What defines the lab

Computer vision with structure and purpose

Public faculty pages point to a lab identity grounded in computer vision, video understanding, robotic vision, 3D modeling, uncertainty estimation, and multimodal learning. Rather than separating geometry from learning, the work treats them as complementary tools for building systems that generalize beyond curated datasets.

Research

Three research directions that connect the group

These themes tie together faculty agendas, student projects, and recent publication signals visible across the group’s public profiles.

01

Video Understanding and Activities of Daily Living

Public pages from Srijan Das, Dominick Reilly, and Arkaprava Sinha show a strong through-line in long-video understanding, action recognition, and multimodal reasoning for activities of daily living.

02

Vision-Language and Vision-Language-Action Models

The student and faculty publication trail highlights VLMs and VLA models that combine RGB video, skeletons, depth, and text for transfer, instruction tuning, and robot policy learning.

03

Reliable 3D and Generative Vision

Hieu Le’s homepage and recent cross-group papers point to a second major pillar: explainable generative modeling, 3D shape reasoning, uncertainty estimation, and controllable vision systems.
